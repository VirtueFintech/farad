Frequently Asked Questions

What are ultracapacitors?

An ultracapacitor, also known as a supercapacitor, or electrochemical capacitor, is a device for storing electrical energy which is growing rapidly in popularity. The design and mechanism of operation is somewhere between an ordinary capacitor and a battery, which opens up some interesting and valuable applications.

What is the difference between capacitor and supercapacitors?

A supercapacitor differs from an ordinary capacitor in two important ways: its plates effectively have a much bigger area and the distance between them is much smaller, because the separator between them works in a different way to a conven-tional dielectric, hence it holds much more capacitance.

Like an ordinary capacitor, a supercapacitor has two plates that are separated. The plates are made from metal coated with a porous substance such as powdery, acti-vated charcoal, which effectively gives them a bigger area for storing much more charge. Imagine electricity is water for a moment: where an ordinary capacitor is like a cloth that can mop up only a tiny little spill, a supercapacitor's porous plates make it more like a chunky sponge that can soak up many times more.

What are some advantages of ultracapacitors over other energy storage and power delivery devices, such as lead-acid batteries?

In addition to safe and reliable operation, ultracapacitors charge and discharge in as little as fractions of a second, perform normally over a broad operating temperature range (-40 to +65 degrees C) and have an operation lifetime of 1 million or more charge/discharge cycles.

What is the Farad supercapacitors primary advantage over its competitors?

There are a few primary advtancges. First, our unique blend of mixed-metal oxide electrode material is intrinsic with higher energy density than our competitors carbon material.

Second, our charging mechanism is both physical (EDLC) and chemical (REDOX), sim-ilar to that of battery chemical reaction, compared our competitors EDLC.

Third, we have a much higher voltage window, a single component cell between 1-24V against our competitors 2.5-2.7V.

Fourth, stacking structure can offer scalable flexible working voltage. Both sides of the UTC’s electrode can be used to form flexible voltages by stacking up electrodes.

Fifth, Our capcacitors are fabricated with DSA technologies , resulting in High Me-chanical Endurance (>50Kg), High Roughness Factor (>10^4), and Corrosion Re-sistance comparable to DSA which translates to Longer life span (up to 3 Millions cycles) against competitors 1 million cycles.

Sixth, our unique patented planar structure enables us to design ultracapacitors with the thickness at 0.25mm (from3-12V) only. It is thin enough to be encapsulated in a smart card as a power source; or to be incorporated with a smart phone battery to upgrade it into a high-power battery capable of delivering 10 amps instant cur-rent.

Can ultracapacitors play a significant role in the future of energy storage?

Ultracapacitors’ high power density, extremely long operational life and reliable per-formance in extreme temperatures give system designers the ability to better satisfy the power requirements of a wide range of demanding applications. With millions of cells and multi-cell modules already powering applications in cars, public transit and other heavy vehicles, wind energy and other industrial applications, ultracapacitors can be expected to play an even more prominent role in the years ahead.
